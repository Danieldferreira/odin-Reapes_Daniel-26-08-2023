Cuban Ropa vieja

Description

tells the story of a poor old man who had nothing to feed his family, so he went and looked in his closets for all the old rags he found, frayed them and made a stew with that. A divinity appeared to him and granted him the miracle of turning the shreds of his rags into meat and, according to what they say, this is the reason why it bears the name "Old Clothes."

However, it was not until a wave of emigrants from the Canary Islands imported this dish to the Americas, specifically to those countries that touch the Caribbean Sea. This is why we find that the dish is preferred in countries like Puerto Rico, Santo Domingo, Venezuela, Colombia, Panama, Costa Rica, and Mexico, but especially Cuba, as it still maintains its original name.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of beef (skirt or flank)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 medium onion, julienned or diced
  • ½ green bell pepper, julienned or diced
  • ½ red bell pepper, julienned or diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, crushed or very finely chopped
  • ½ cup tomato sauce
  • ½ cup dry white wine
  • ½ cup beef broth
  • ½ tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon of oregano
  • Salt and pepper
  • Oil
  • Water

Steps

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add flank steak and cook until browned, about 4 minutes per side.
  2. Transfer steak to a slow cooker; pour in beef broth and tomato sauce. Add tomato paste, bell pepper, onion, garlic, cumin, cilantro, olive oil, and vinegar; stir until well blended.
  3. Cover and cook on Low for up to 10 hours, or on High for 4 hours
  4. Shred steak in the slow cooker with two forks
